Digestive Disease Week® (DDW) 2018 is only a few short weeks away! It’s time to start planning your itinerary for DDW in Washington, D.C. The AGA Institute Council sections have highlighted their “must-see” AGA sessions at the meeting. In addition, each section will recognize their Research Mentor Awardee for 2018. Established in 2010, the Research Mentor Award recognizes council members for their achievements as outstanding mentors in specific areas of research.

For additional information about AGA programming at DDW, view the AGA Highlights at DDW 2018 booklet .

All presentations will take place in the Walter E. Washington Convention Center.

Imaging, Endoscopy & Advanced Technology

Session Title: Infection Transmission in Gastrointestinal Endoscopy
Session Date: Sunday, June 3
Session Time: 10–11:30 a.m.
Why you Should Attend: The recent and unfortunate occurrences of transmission of infection in gastrointestinal endoscopy, especially ERCP, have led to widespread public awareness, litigation and a re-examination of our reprocessing practices and guidelines. This session seeks to address the basis of bacterial transmission; quality assurance efforts and monitoring for occult transmission; physician liability and risk management; and society’s response to standards of reprocessing.

Session Title: Advances in Endoscopic Diagnosis and Staging of Inflammatory Bowel Disease
Session Date: Sunday, June 3
Session Time: 4–5:30 p.m.
Why you Should Attend: Clinical manifestations of inflammatory bowel disease don’t always follow endoscopic or histological degree of inflammation. In fact, they almost never do. In the modern era, direct visual inspection by endoscopy, with or without tissue acquisition for pathological evaluation, may no longer be the best method for diagnosing and staging IBD. This session will review a variety of technological advances that when applied to inflammatory bowel disease have the potential to change our practice.

Session Title: Advanced Endoscopy of the Biliary Tract
Session Date: Saturday, June 2
Session Time: 2–3:30 p.m.
Why you Should Attend: Over the past few years, interventional endoscopists have been inundated with an array of tools and methods to improve the diagnosis of biliary disease. This session seeks to compare and contrast some of the commonly used endoscopic techniques for tissue sampling as well as examine how newer imaging technologies such as cholangioscopy, confocal endoscopy and optical coherence tomography may be applied to biliary disease.

Session Title: Advances in Imaging of Pancreatic Disease
Session Date: Saturday, June 2
Session Time: 8–9:30 a.m.
Why you Should Attend: Technological achievements have always preceded advances in clinical outcomes. In many ways, technological improvements in medical imaging are among the most relevant to our specialty. This session will highlight not only the most important imaging advances of 2017-2018, but it will also provide a glimpse into the future of pancreatic imaging.

Research Mentor Award
The award will be presented to Michael B. Wallace, MD, MPH, AGAF, during the session, Advances in Imaging and Therapy of the Pancreas and Biliary System on Saturday, June 2, 4-5:30 p.m., 4:28-4:34 p.m. in Room144.
